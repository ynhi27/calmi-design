How Research Shaped Our Design

Our original concept changed significantly based on what we learned from users.

Iteration 1 — Core Modality

Before Research

Pure Voice Bot

Our original concept was a voice-first chatbot that could optionally be trained on voice recordings of the deceased — allowing users to "hear" their loved one again. We believed voice would create the most emotionally resonant experience.

After Interviews

Text-First + Optional Voice

User interviews revealed strong discomfort with simulated voices of deceased loved ones. Participants described it as unsettling rather than comforting, raising ethical concerns. We pivoted to a text-based companion with optional voice support using a neutral AI voice — preserving accessibility without imitation.

Key insight: The desire to hear a loved one's voice is real — but recreating it artificially can feel invasive rather than comforting. Users wanted presence, not imitation.

Iteration 2 — Core Purpose

Before Research

Grief Healing Tool

Early framing positioned CALMI as a tool to help users "move through" or "overcome" grief — with a therapeutic arc toward resolution and emotional closure.

After Survey

Remembrance Companion

Survey data showed that 78% of respondents still actively wanted to speak to the person they lost — even years later. Grief intensity averaged 4.2/10 regardless of time elapsed. This reframed our purpose: grief is not something to close, but to carry. CALMI became a remembrance companion, not a healing tool.

Key insight: Young people don't want to "get over" grief — they want ongoing connection. This fundamentally changed our product philosophy.

Iteration 3 — Accessibility & Tone

Before Research

General Emotional Support

Initial designs assumed users would proactively seek support and be open to guided exercises and resource referrals — similar to existing mental health apps.

After Survey

Low-pressure, No-judgment Design

56% of respondents had never sought professional support. Top barriers were cost, discomfort with vulnerability, and stigma. We redesigned the conversation flow to remove all pressure — no check-ins, no assessments, no required inputs. CALMI only asks; it never pushes.

Key insight: The biggest barrier to grief support is not lack of tools — it's the emotional cost of asking for help. CALMI must feel like talking to yourself, not being evaluated.
