Background & Significance

The Problem

Grief is a universal human experience — yet it remains one of the most underserved areas in mental health support, especially for teenagers and young adults. During critical years of psychological development, the loss of a significant person can compound challenges and lead to long-term emotional difficulties if left unaddressed.

The Gap

Despite the prevalence of grief among young people, many don't seek help. Barriers include social stigma, discomfort with vulnerability, and the cost of professional services. Most existing digital tools are not designed for youth, nor do they focus on remembrance as a healthy ongoing practice.

Our Approach

Through surveys (n=23) and user interviews, we learned that 78% of young adults still wish they could talk to the person they lost — even years later. CALMI was built around this insight: grief isn't something to close, it's something to carry with care.

85% of respondents had experienced significant loss · 56% had never sought professional support · Average grief intensity: 4.2 / 10 — regardless of how long ago the loss occurred.
